Security is the foundational pillar of DotMarket. Because the protocol operates fully autonomously on the Arc Testnet handling rapid 1-minute prediction cycles and substantial pool liquidity, maintaining an impenetrable security posture is absolutely critical. DotMarket has been engineered from the ground up to be trustless, non-custodial, and highly resistant to all known decentralized finance (DeFi) attack vectors.

This document outlines our comprehensive security model, how we protect user funds, the transparency of our smart contracts, and how specific vulnerabilities have been systematically neutralized.

## Non-Custodial Architecture

The most critical security feature of DotMarket is its non-custodial design. Unlike centralized exchanges or traditional prediction platforms where a company holds your assets, DotMarket never takes ownership of your funds. 

When you place a prediction, your tokens are transferred directly into an immutable smart contract escrow on the Arc Testnet. The rules governing the release of these funds are hardcoded into the contract logic. There are no "admin keys," backdoors, or privileged functions that allow the development team or any third party to pause the contract and drain the liquidity pools. The only way funds can leave the contract is through the mathematical resolution of a prediction round or via the automated refund mechanism in the event of an oracle failure or tie.

  Don't trust, verify. You can inspect all of your transactions and the exact balances of the DotMarket smart contracts at any time using the Arc Testnet block explorer. Your funds are always completely transparent on-chain.
</Callout>

The deployed smart contracts on the Arc Testnet are also fully verified, meaning the bytecode running on the blockchain perfectly matches the human-readable source code published in our repositories.

## Mitigating Common Attack Vectors

The DotMarket architecture has been specifically designed to neutralize the most prevalent attack vectors in the DeFi space.

### Flash Loan Attacks
Flash loans allow attackers to borrow massive amounts of capital uncollateralized, provided they return the funds within the same transaction. Attackers often use flash loans to manipulate on-chain Automated Market Maker (AMM) prices and exploit protocols relying on those AMMs.
**Mitigation:** DotMarket is completely immune to flash loan price manipulation because it does not derive its Entry or Settlement prices from on-chain AMMs like Uniswap. All price data is sourced from the off-chain Pyth Network, which cannot be manipulated by on-chain flash loans.

### Frontrunning and MEV
Miner Extractable Value (MEV) and frontrunning occur when a bot monitors the mempool (pending transactions) and pays higher gas fees to execute their transaction before yours, often exploiting a price change.
**Mitigation:** DotMarket mitigates MEV through strict timestamp enforcement. The moment a 60-second Prediction phase ends, the contract hard-rejects any further entries. Even if a bot attempts to frontrun the Oracle Settlement transaction after seeing a price swing, the contract will simply revert their prediction because the entry window for that round has permanently closed.

### Oracle Manipulation
If an attacker can manipulate the oracle, they can artificially guarantee a winning outcome for their prediction.
**Mitigation:** As detailed in our Oracle System documentation, Pyth Network aggregates from over 90 institutional sources. Manipulating this robust consensus requires a globally coordinated attack across dozens of traditional and decentralized exchanges simultaneously, making it practically impossible.

## Smart Contract Upgradability

Many DeFi protocols use proxy patterns that allow the development team to upgrade the smart contract code at will. While useful for rapid iteration, this introduces a significant centralization risk, as a compromised admin key could push a malicious upgrade that drains the pools.

DotMarket takes a vastly more secure approach. The core prediction engine contracts are **non-upgradeable**. Once deployed, the code can never be altered. 

If the DotMarket team wishes to introduce new features, optimize gas consumption, or deploy a new version of the protocol, we must deploy entirely new smart contracts. Users must then consciously and voluntarily migrate their liquidity to the new version. This immutable design guarantees that the rules of the protocol you interact with today will be the exact same rules tomorrow. 

<Callout type="warning" title="Phishing and Impersonation">
  While our smart contracts are mathematically secure, you must remain vigilant against social engineering. Always ensure you are interacting with the official DotMarket frontend and never share your wallet's seed phrase. The protocol will never ask for your private keys.
</Callout>
